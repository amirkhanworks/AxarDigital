SEBI PIT Amendment 2025 — What Every Listed Company Needs to Know

SEBI amended the PIT Regulations 2015 in March 2025. The changes take effect June 2025 and require active compliance reassessment across every listed company and SEBI-registered intermediary in India.

Download Free Readiness Checklist Request a Demo

Three changes that require your attention before June 2025

Broadened Scope

Broader UPSI definition

The amendment broadens the categories of information that qualify as Unpublished Price Sensitive Information. Compliance teams must review their existing UPSI identification lists against the amended definition and update their SDD accordingly.

Action required: UPSI list review
Intermediaries

Strengthened Regulation 9A

Stricter requirements on UPSI sharing protocols for SEBI-registered intermediaries. Information barrier controls, deal-level UPSI tracking, and employee Designated Person monitoring are now more explicitly required under the amended provisions.

Action required: Intermediary UPSI audit
Records Management

Enhanced SDD requirements

The SDD obligations under Regulation 3(5) are reinforced. 8-year retention remains mandatory. Hosting on spreadsheets or shared drives creates direct liability for the Compliance Officer and does not meet the access-controlled, timestamped standard required.

Action required: SDD infrastructure review

Why this matters: recent SEBI enforcement

These are not hypothetical risks. SEBI has taken enforcement action against named individuals at major Indian companies in 2024 and 2025.

₹25 lakh penalty — Infosys CEO (2024)

SEBI imposed a ₹25 lakh penalty on the Infosys CEO for inadequate controls under the PIT Regulations. The case established that senior executives bear personal liability for PIT framework failures, not just the compliance function. No size of company is exempt from enforcement.

Warning letter — Nestle India Compliance Officer (April 2025)

SEBI issued a formal warning letter to Nestle India's compliance officer for an insider trading breach. Warning letters precede enforcement orders. The April 2025 timing — immediately before the June amendment effective date — signals active regulatory scrutiny of PIT controls across listed companies.
